Question One:
Your interpretation of what each of the 10 principles in the article mean.

Good Design is:

  • Innovative:
    explores what is needed and wanted during it’s time

  • Practical:
    designed to do what it’s supposed to

  • Aesthetically pleasing:
    using forms in a way that help it best serve its purpose while making it pleasing to look at

  • Understandable:
    not complicated and easy to understand without too much thought. It’s simple

  • Unobtrusive:
    Allows the users to feel comfort and a sense of self expression.

  • Honest:
    Doesn’t add unneeded bells and whistles people don’t need. Cost is fair and based off the needs of the product.

  • Long Lasting:
    Object is sustainable. It is meant to last the consumer long periods of time as opposed to cheaper products that have shorter lifespans.

  • Well thought out:
    Well researched and thoughtful.

  • Environmentally Friendly:
    mindfulness of materials and lifecycles of products.

  • Simple:
    Doesn’t overcomplicate the product.

    • This article aims to show that design in not something that has to be flashy and overcomplicated. It is a process that allows for design to cater to the time period in which it’s made, the users, and functionality. All 10 principles of design work together to make design that is more than just art or short-erm products.

Question Two:
An example of something you use that follows each principle as you interpret it.

    Based off this reading I would consider my favorite drawing utensil as good design. It’s a very simple object yet it serves it purpose perfectly. When looking into the smaller details of the design it allows for portability and a comfortable grip. The cap also has a clip that is sturdier than most pens and can attach the end of the pen. Each pen is also easily identified based of the marks along the sides.
